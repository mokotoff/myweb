Randy Angella Double Top Guitar Construction

This is the setup for thinning out the top layer from the underside.

This is the nomes cell in top of the top veneer. It's glued to the bottom of the top veneer. .

This is a sanding disc that is tapered at the bottom to get the rise from .025 to .085 at the edge. It only has sandpaper on the tapered outer 1 inch so it doesn't sand the flat .025. Just the taper.

This is the nomex being sanded flat for the bottom veneer.

This a shot of the fans with the carbon fiber center core.

This is a sheet of acetate that has numerous spots of double sided tape. It's laid onto the nomex that's cut to fit the cedar insert that supports the rosette and that portion of the top that has the fretboard over it. The acetate and nomex are carefully lifted off the upside down top veneer and laid onto a sheet of glass that has glue rolled on to it. Then replaced and placed in a vacuum bag and left overnight.

This is the nomex glued to the underside of the top veneer. The top veneer is concave with the edge tapered from .025 1 inch from the edge to .085 at the edge. The next photo is how the nomex is sanded flat to accept the bottom .020 veneer.
